## Insights Used
## Insight 1:
Extreme Greed days recorded the highest average trader profitability (67.89) and the highest win rate (46.49%), indicating that traders generally perform better during periods of strong positive market sentiment.
## Insight 2: 
Extreme Fear days produced the lowest win rate (37.06%) and one of the lowest average PnL values (34.54), suggesting that negative market sentiment is associated with increased trading risk and lower profitability.
## Insight 3: 
Fear days had a higher average trade size (~USD 7,816) than Greed days (~USD 5,737), yet achieved a lower win rate. This indicates that simply increasing position size does not improve trading outcomes, especially during unfavorable market conditions.

## Strategy 1: Sentiment-Based Position Sizing
Based on Insight 1 & Insight 2

   ## Rule of Thumb:
During Extreme Greed days, increase position size by 10–15% only for traders with a recent positive win rate (>45%). During Fear and Extreme Fear days, reduce position size by 20–30% and prioritize capital preservation.

   ## Reason:
Extreme Greed recorded the highest average PnL (67.89) and win rate (46.49%), whereas Extreme Fear showed the lowest win rate (37.06%) and significantly lower profitability.

## Strategy 2: Adaptive Risk Management

Based on Insight 2 & Insight 3

   ## Rule of Thumb:
During Fear and Extreme Fear days, reduce leverage and avoid increasing trade size, especially for high-leverage or inconsistent traders. During Greed and Extreme Greed days, maintain normal leverage and allow slightly higher capital allocation only for consistently profitable traders.

   ## Reason:
Fear days had the largest average trade size (~USD 7,816) but still produced lower win rates than Extreme Greed, indicating that larger positions alone do not improve performance. Better results come from disciplined risk management combined with favorable market sentiment.
